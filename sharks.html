Sharks

Sharks are ancient and highly successful predatory fish characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, multiple gill slits, and rows of replaceable teeth. They play a crucial role as apex predators in most marine ecosystems, helping to regulate the populations of their prey. There are over 500 species, ranging from the small, deep-sea lantern shark to the massive, filter-feeding whale shark. Despite their fearsome reputation, very few species pose a threat to humans, and many are vulnerable to overfishing.
