Fish

Fish are aquatic, gill-bearing vertebrates that lack limbs with digits. They represent the most diverse group of vertebrates, with over 30,000 species found in nearly all aquatic environments, from high mountain streams to the deepest ocean trenches. They are broadly categorized into three main groups: jawless fish, cartilaginous fish (like sharks), and bony fish, which make up the vast majority. Fish are essential to the global food chain and vital for many human economies.
