Dolphins

Dolphins are highly intelligent and social marine mammals belonging to the order Cetacea. Known for their agility and playful behavior, they use a sophisticated system of clicks and whistles called echolocation to navigate and hunt. They live in groups called pods, exhibiting complex social structures and cooperative feeding techniques. Most species inhabit the warmer waters of the continental shelves, though some, like the orca (a type of oceanic dolphin), live in colder seas.
