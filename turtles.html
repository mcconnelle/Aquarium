Sea Turtles are ancient reptiles that have roamed the oceans for over 100 million years. They are easily distinguished from land tortoises by their large, streamlined shells and powerful flippers, which are adapted for swimming long distances. All seven species of sea turtles are considered vulnerable or endangered. They undertake epic migrations between their feeding grounds and the beaches where the females return to lay their eggs, often navigating back to the exact beach where they were born.
